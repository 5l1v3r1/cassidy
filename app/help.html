Help

Overview

Cassidy generates unique passwords by combining a service, key, and phrase. Add your own phrase under the Settings tab. A key will be generated pseudo-randomly for you.

Write it down or remember your key! You must have it to recreate the same passwords.

Services

Recent services appear on the left side. When typing in the service field, you may select from recent services using the autocomplete dropdown.

Saving services

When entering a service, use Enter or Tab to save the service in the sidebar.

A saved service will also save all the current settings with it. Selecting a saved service will repopulate the settings that it was created with. For example, if you added a service requiring 15 characters and no symbols, this will be reapplied automatically when selected.

Removing services

Hover over a service in the sidebar and click the × on the right side.

Settings

You may influence the creation of a password by turning on or off several options, including lowercase letters, capital letters, numbers, dashes, sumbols, and length.

Key

Your key makes your passwords unique. No one else can have the same generated password for a given service + key combination.

Phrase

Unlike the settings and key, your phrase is not saved with a service. It will be encrypted and persisted in localStorage for your convenience.

Syncing with Dropbox

If you connect to Dropbox, your services will be saved to Dropbox instead of localStorage. This means that you can connect several devices (computers, phones, tablets) and have the same services available everywhere.

Cassidy does not save passwords, only most of the information required to recreate passwords. Each service will have settings data attached, including the key. You must reenter your Phrase on every new device.
